# Decision 002 — SOS closure sprint v1.0 (DRAFT)

- **Date**: 2026-04-19
- **Status**: DRAFT — pending acks from codex, kasra, hermes
- **Scope**: `squad:mumega-launch`
- **Plan doc**: `docs/plans/2026-04-19-sos-closure-v1.md`

## Decision

Run a **9-day closure sprint** ending in SOS v1.0.0, then freeze the SOS
substrate for 3-6 months while the squad focuses on product (mumega.com,
Inkwell template, first paying tenants). No patching during the freeze —
only CVE fixes and production incidents.

## Rationale

Hadi's directive 2026-04-19:

> "Make full plan … I want to never open SOS for a while and focus on
> product. Do not patch."

The substrate has 80% of its contracts built. The remaining 20% is
wiring + cleanup + a deliberate seal. Shipping v1.0 with the seal
forces the team to use SOS as a *product substrate*, not a thing they
patch every time something breaks. It also gives the storefront and
Inkwell template the stable foundation they need.

## Three layers (non-negotiable)

- **SOS microkernel** — bus, squad, mesh, economy, gate, identity (loom)
- **Inkwell microkernel** — template + tenant runtime (kasra)
- **Mumega storefront** — mumega.com + marketplace + brand (codex)

Hermes is the operator layer: runs prod routing + deploy gates + D1 state.

## Tier ownership

- **Tier 1 — Substrate hardening** (loom): bus envelope, XACK, mesh gaps
- **Tier 2 — Squad primitives + git-backed memory** (loom, inspired by egregore-labs/egregore)
- **Tier 3 — Storefront + marketplace product** (codex, kasra-assisted)
- **Tier 4 — Prod ops + cutover** (hermes, loom-assisted)
