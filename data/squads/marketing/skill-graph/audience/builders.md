# Audience: Builders

## Who They Are
AI engineers, indie hackers, SaaS founders, open-source devs, agency owners building automation. They ship code. They understand terminals. They've tried LangChain and found it lacking. Revenue range $0-$100k/mo.

## What They Want
- Architecture breakdowns they can replicate
- Real configs, real YAML, real code — not pseudocode
- Cost analysis: "how much does this actually cost to run?"
- Comparative analysis: "why a bus instead of a framework?"
- Open-source tools they can fork and use today

## How to Talk to Them
- Peer energy. we're building alongside them
- Show the git diff, not the press release
- Technical specificity: "Redis XADD to stream, pub/sub for wake" not "message queue"
- Challenge: "if you're still using a framework, here's why a kernel is better"
